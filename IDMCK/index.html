About Us

About Us

The Inclusive disabilty mainstreaming consortium- Foundation(IDMC-K) is a national charity deductible gift recipient (DGR) public benevolent institution committed to providing life-transforming services that enable Orphaned Children with Disabilties (OcwDs) to increase their independence and achieve their goals in life Including promote excellence in the fields of learning disabilties, health, education and the arts & humanities. Our funds are generated through an endowment, all on the basis of expert peer review. We work with over 4,000 children and adults with physical or learning disabilities or acquired brain injuries. Whether it’s gaining new skills to live independently, rehabilitation after a brain injury or stroke, or improving independence through increased mobility and accessible holidays; (IDMC-K) helps disabled people to fulfil their potential in life.

Funding

Grants are generally given for capital infrastructure (new build, refurbishment and equipment) supporting excellence in the fields of disability policy mainstreaming for Orphaned Children with Disabilties (OcwDs) and are all bursary, scholarship or salary schemes focussed on people rather than buildings or equipment. Our Project

Problem Statement

There is an urgent need for interventions for and by OCWDs to be evidence-based and firmly rooted in proven approaches. This is critical in ensuring that Orphaned Children with Disabilities (OCWDs) are actively supported, via inclusivity and champion their plight especially during this festive christmas season inter-alia positive structural responses. Unfortunately, in Kenya there is lack of accomodatve support on the extent of exclusion of Orphaned Children with disabilities in societal structures and evidence of programs/initiatives that work to enhance inclusion. Lack of this data and information impedes understanding of the extent of exclusion and the subsequent action needed to remove disabling barriers for OCwD to be embraced in society. Due to difficulties in communication, mobility, interacting with others, learning and lack of support to participate in society Orphaned Children with disabilities are usually left out thus suffer double tragedy.

Project Description.

This project addresses the above problems by ensuring that, for the first time in Kenya, this christmas season Orphaned Children with Disabilities (OCWDs) are supported by corporate organization and accomodated to abhore (exclusion) of Orphaned Children with Disabilities (OCWDs) and are not left out in social and curicular participation and what works for their inclusion.

Target Beneficiaries

The direct beneficiaries will be Orphaned Children with Disabilities (OCWDs) especially diverse disabilities aged Infants to 16 years. These include youths with hearing, visual and physical Impairment, cerebral palsy, epilepsy, Down syndrome, autistic spectrum disorder, dwarfism, albinism, intellectual & cognitive handicap, emotional & behavioral Disorders and those with complex and multiple disabilities such as deaf blindness. Through this project, Orphaned Children with Disabilities (OCWDs) will benefit from enhanced inclusion and support by the corporates leading to realization of their rights to accessing services and participation. The project will directly engage with Orphaned Children with Disabilities (OCWDs) with varied disabilities, accomodation support this christmas season and advocacy for their own issues will be enhanced. Indirect beneficiaries will include:

  • Children without disabilities and community members whose knowledge on inclusion of people with disabilities will be improved;
  • Civil society organizations whose knowledge of the extent of exclusion of Orphaned Children with Disabilities (OCWDs) will be also be improved affecting how they design programs for people with disabilities;
  • Government officials whose understanding for inclusion of Orphaned Children with Disabilities (OCWDs) will also be improved leading to better services for (OCWDs) .

Our Goal

The goal of this project is to support inclusion and participation in society of Orphaned Children with Disabilities (OCWDs) this christmas season through corporate organizations under institutional CSR, evidence-based information, action and advocacy.

Planning

Specifically the project seeks to generate evidence generation on the extent to which of Orphaned Children with Disabilities (OCWDs) are excluded from the society and corporate player despite CSR/CSI.
